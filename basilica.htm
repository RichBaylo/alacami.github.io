The Alacami   

The Basilica



The site of the tomb formed the focus for the second discernible phase of activity on the site (late 5th / early 6th century), the construction of a three-aisled basilica, with galleries, a clerestory and a slightly projecting apse. To the west end a narthex, porch and staircase were added at a slightly later date. Access to the hypogeum after the construction of the church was afforded by means of a passageway leading from the west steps.
 
 
 


The exterior walls of the basilica are decorated with a base-moulding, a hood-moulding and a cornice, in a style typical of the Antioch region during the late 5th and 6th centuries.

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Exterior porches extended along the west and north sides and to the south lay a large irregular courtyard, entered from both east and west sides. Excavation has revealed extensive mosaic coverage, both inside and outside the building.
