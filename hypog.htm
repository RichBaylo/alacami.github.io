The Alacami   

The Hypogeum


From the opening in the west wall a set of steps lead down into the burial chamber. Low benches flank the north and south walls and four earthenware pipes open into the centre of the vaulted roof, above a deep basin in the plaster floor between the benches. The pipes would seem to have been incorporated to allow ritual libation from above ground, provisions for which were very common in Roman funerary practice. At the east end of the chamber a separate basin is formed by the construction of a low partitioning wall between the flanking benches.
