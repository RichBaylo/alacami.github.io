    The basilica was richly adorned with mosaics, both inside and out, some of which remain well preserved. Many were exposed and recorded by Professor Çambel in 1960 (photo) and their continued preservation will be the concern of a future project. The mosaics in the courtyard, to the east of the apse and in the narthex all bear geometric designs, while Professor Çambel observed that the scant remains of the nave pavement indicated the presence of opus sectile and opus alexandrinum. Only fragments remain of the mosaic in the north aisle, yet the well preserved south aisle and side chamber mosaics are beautifully executed and feature faunal scenes.

    These illustrations are taken from paintings of the mosaics produced subsequent to the 1960 excavations by Halet Çambel and are included with her permission.
